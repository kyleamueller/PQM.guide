## Remarks

`Number.BitwiseShiftRight` shifts the binary representation of `number` to the right by `shift` positions. High-order bits are filled based on the sign of the original value (arithmetic right shift). Shifting right by `n` is equivalent to integer division by 2^n.

This is useful for extracting packed fields from integer values, scaling down bit-encoded data, or working with binary-encoded flags.

## Examples

### Example 1: Shift 16 right by 2 (divide by 4)

```powerquery
Number.BitwiseShiftRight(16, 2)
```

<!--output
{"columns":[{"name":"Result","type":"number"}],"rows":[{"Result":4}]}
-->

### Example 2: Extract high nibble (upper 4 bits) from a byte

```powerquery
Number.BitwiseShiftRight(0b10110101, 4)
```

<!--output
{"columns":[{"name":"Result","type":"number"}],"rows":[{"Result":11}]}
-->

### Example 3: Undo a left shift

```powerquery
let
    Original = 5,
    Shifted = Number.BitwiseShiftLeft(Original, 3),
    Restored = Number.BitwiseShiftRight(Shifted, 3)
in
    Restored
```
