## Remarks

`Number.Permutations` computes P(n, k) — the number of ordered arrangements of `permutationSize` items drawn from a set of `setSize` items. Unlike combinations, the order of selection matters here: choosing A then B is different from choosing B then A.

The formula is: P(n, k) = n! / (n − k)!

`Number.Permutations(n, k)` is always >= `Number.Combinations(n, k)` (by a factor of k!). When `permutationSize` equals `setSize`, the result is n! (all possible orderings of the full set).

## Examples

### Example 1: Ordered arrangements of 3 items chosen from 5

```powerquery
Number.Permutations(5, 3)
```
