## Remarks

`Number.BitwiseShiftLeft` shifts the binary representation of `number` to the left by `shift` positions, filling the vacated low-order bits with zeros. Shifting left by `n` is equivalent to multiplying by 2^n (for values that do not overflow).

This is commonly used in low-level data encoding, packing multiple values into a single integer field, or constructing bitmasks.

## Examples

### Example 1: Shift 1 left by 3 (multiply by 8)

```powerquery
Number.BitwiseShiftLeft(1, 3)
```
